We’re so excited that more than 300 people have registered for this event! You all bring so much awesomeness to the table. But because of such a great turnout, the event organizers are going to be very busy. You can help by reading this document and sharing information with your teammates.

Also, in person, look for volunteers who have special color-coded name tags. They’re happy to help! Additionally, the Sketch City organizers of this event are Jeff Reichman, Neeraj Tandon, Randy Baxley, and Meredith Maines. Share your favorite moments using #houhackathon2017

Schedule

Saturday

  • 9 AM Jump start your hackathon with coffee and breakfast bites while meeting potential teammates. Discuss ideas big and small or get started right away with your team to bring your idea to life!
  • 11 AM Doors are officially open for the hackathon as speakers and City of Houston employees stream in to pitch additional ideas.
  • 12 PM Welcome, announcements, and words of advice are shared in the main room. Additional ideas will be pitched. After this kick-off, we should be in teams and get to working on our projects!
  • 12:30 PM Lunch is served.
  • 7 PM Dinner is served.
  • 10:30 PM Late-night snack is served.
  • 12 AM The building's doors will lock, but you may continue to stay to work. All that will change is if you step out, your teammates inside the building will need to come let you back in.

Sunday

  • 7 AM The building's doors will reopen.
  • 7:30 AM Grab coffee and bagels from the food area.
  • 12 PM While you can continue working on the project and the presentation, the project form with project description and a link to the presentation must be submitted online by this time.

Code of Conduct

Also, we we want everyone to feel welcome, safe, and free to work on civic projects with teams or on their own at the hackathon and all Sketch City events. Please read and abide by the code of conduct. If you feel uncomfortable at any time during the hackathon, please come find one of the organizers so we can help. Or email us at info@sketchcity.org, and we’ll get right back to you.

Submitting Your Project

Your work must be accessible via the internet, and a link to your project and presentation must be submitted using Devpost by 12 PM Sunday. We recommend registering for Devpost early and assembling your team.

There is only one presentation computer. You will not be allowed to use your own computer or transfer any files with external drives or connect other media, etc. Your presentation and/or project demo must live on the internet and must be linked to through your Devpost submission.

Because there will be potentially 30+ presentations, we want to minimize transitions between teams (since that can add up to an hour or more of lag time). Please respect your fellow hackathon-ers by being prepared.

Presenting your project

Each team has three minutes to present their work. To help you stay on track, we’ll be displaying a digital timer. We’ll signal your cut off at the end of three minutes. It’s nothing personal; we just need to respect all the teams and our judges.

Your work must be accessible via the internet, and the link must be posted on Devpost. Here are some ways that people present hackathon projects:

  • Through a presentation (using something like SlideShare or Google Slides with a shareable URL)

  • Through a screencast or video (there are lots of tools you can use, such as Quickcast)

  • Through a live product demo on your web-based project.

If you build a mobile app, you will not have the opportunity to connect the device to the projector. We recommend making a video instead.

It’s helpful to have one main presenter who is responsible for hitting all of the bullet points and managing the clock. You are welcome to have multiple presenters, however.

Judging

There are a handful of judges from the Houston community. They will be evaluating your project based on:

  • Relevance. Is this solving a need, answering a question, serving citizens, or otherwise making an impact?

  • Ambition. How big is the challenge that the project addresses?

  • Progress. How far did the team get? Working demos are better than static images.

  • Presentation. Did the team present their work in a compelling way? Was it easy to understand the project, the scope, and how far they got?

Awards

The prizes are:
